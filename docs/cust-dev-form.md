# Scorpius — Customer Development Form

> **Purpose:** validate that Uzbek school students (grades 5–11) (a) regularly experience the
> "I don't understand my homework / I got a bad mark" problem, (b) currently solve it with weak
> workarounds (or not at all), and (c) would actually use a swipeable AI tutor that follows their
> emaktab progress. Target: **100+ responses** via team + friend Telegram channels in 3 hours.
>
> **Style:** Rob Fitzpatrick's *Mom Test* — every question asks about **past behaviour** (last
> week, last month, real numbers), never about hypothetical liking or pitching the idea. The
> only forward-looking question is Q6, framed in concrete daily-use terms.
>
> **Language:** Uzbek-primary (polite *siz* form). English translation under each item for the
> judges' file.
>
> **Distribution:** Telegram channels (team + friend's network) → student/pupil audience.

---

## Copy-paste to Google Forms in 7 minutes

1. Open https://forms.google.com → **Blank form**
2. Set the title and description (block A).
3. For each question (Q1–Q7) below: click `+` → set type (in **bold** at the top) → paste the
   Uzbek question text → paste the options. Mark **required** where noted.
4. Settings → **Responses** → enable "Collect email addresses" = **OFF** (lowers friction). Link
   responses to a new Sheet for live analysis.
5. End-of-form message (block Z) → paste into Settings → Presentation → "Confirmation message".
6. Click **Send** → copy the short link → ship to team + friend channels.

## What we'll quote in the pitch (when responses ≥ 50)

From the form, the three pitch-ready stats we extract:

1. **Problem frequency:** "{X}% of students said they had ≥3 unintelligible-homework moments
   last week." → Q2 sum of (3–5) + (6+) divided by total.
2. **Workaround weakness:** "{Y}% currently use **YouTube or nothing** — no personalized layer."
   → Q3 share of YouTube-only or 'nothing' responses.
3. **Intent + conversion:** "{Z} students want to try it tomorrow; {N} left Telegram usernames
   in 90 seconds." → Q6 yes-count + Q7 fill rate.

These three numbers go on slides 2, 3, and 8 of the pitch deck respectively.
