# Beta Tester Quick-Start — Telegram Message + 3 Feedback Questions

> **Purpose:** ship the beta link to 5–15 real Uzbek school students (friend's brother +
> classmates, friends-of-friends) **in the next 60–90 min**, harvest 2–3 quotable lines
> for the pitch traction slide (Slide 8: the "It actually explained it" quote slot).
>
> **Strategy:** small group, fast turnaround. Don't ask for long surveys — ask for ONE
> specific behavioral test ("pick a topic you don't get, try it, tell us what you felt"),
> then THREE questions. Voice-message answers are 10× more honest than typed surveys.
>
> **Distribution path:**
> 1. Friend's brother → his Telegram class group (5–10 kids in one shot).
> 2. Each beta student gets a personal DM saying "this is the founder, your honest feedback
>    matters" — closes the loop emotionally.
> 3. Reply in the next 60 min counts → quote goes into pitch slide 8.

## Harvesting protocol — for the pitch slide

When responses come in, look for these patterns and pick **the 2–3 quotes that go on
Slide 8** of the deck:

**Strong quote = any line that:**
- Compares us to school/teacher ("better than my teacher", "school doesn't explain this")
- Names a specific moment of understanding ("I finally got fractions", "the diagram clicked")
- Says they'd use it tomorrow ("I'd use this every night", "wish I had this for the exam")
- Surprises them ("I didn't know learning could feel like this")

**Weak quote = avoid these:**
- Generic praise ("nice", "good", "cool")
- Vague ("interesting", "useful")
- Feature requests instead of feeling ("you should add X")

**Action when a strong quote arrives:**
1. DM the student: "Can I use this exact sentence with your first name + grade in our
   pitch today?"
2. On YES → edit `docs/pitch/index.html` slide 8, replace the placeholder quote with the
   real one + their name + grade
3. Take a screenshot of the Telegram message itself — keep as proof for the judges if asked

---

## Time-boxed expectations

- **0–15 min after blast:** 2–4 people click the link (10–30% click rate on first wave)
- **15–45 min:** 1–3 people actually test and reply
- **45–90 min:** if you have 2 quotable responses → stop, you have enough for the pitch
- **If you have 0 responses by T-90min before pitch:** revert pitch slide 8 to placeholder
  quote ("Beta student, 7-sinf · 2026-05-24"), don't fake names

Honesty over performance. Judges can smell a fake quote 100 km away.
