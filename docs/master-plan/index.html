From 11 lessons in a quiz wrapper to a learning platform a child cannot put down.

A phased build plan synthesized from five parallel deep-research passes — Khanmigo / Koji / Synthesis tutor SOTA, the obsession-engineering literature, mastery-learning & knowledge tracing, content production at scale, and 1-2 hour session design. Each phase is shippable. Each phase has a build list, an exit criterion, and a KPI. The end-state isn't more features — it's the architecture below.

I
The architecture · six pillars

What a next-gen learning platform actually is.

Every successful learning app — Khanmigo, Koji, Synthesis, Brilliant, Duolingo, Prodigy — sits on the same six pillars. Today Scorpius has thin versions of three. Building the missing three (and upgrading the weak three) is the master plan.

Pillar 01 · Pedagogy engine
Mastery, not minutes.
A real per-skill mastery model — BKT + half-life recall — running on every attempt. The next problem is picked at P(correct) ≈ 0.85 (Wilson & Shenhav's 85% rule). The constellation only ignites a star when the skill is mastered in mixed-context, not on the same screen it was taught. Today: hardcoded "density" in weakest.ts:37, streak = 1 in SkyView.tsx:336.
Pillar 02 · Koji-grade in-canvas AI tutor
Humo sees the sim and draws on it.
Not a chat-sidebar tutor (Khanmigo). A tutor that has read access to the live sim state and write access to the canvas: highlight(), annotate(), poseSubProblem(), setSliderTarget(). At page arrival it narrates what changed. On a wrong answer, it never says "wrong" — it says "I got a different answer — walk me through your step." Today: chat-only, generic LLM, no sim awareness.
Pillar 03 · Lab simulations as instruments
Sandboxes, not quiz with sliders.
Kill every onComplete?.() win-condition baked into the 13 sims today. Replace with a persistent Lab Notebook that records every parameter the student varied. PhET's freedom is the only thing Scorpius is provably behind on — average sim freedom score 2.1/5. Sandbox mode, draggable protractor, time-series graphs on the 3 strongest sims first.
Pillar 04 · Obsession architecture
Variable rewards, named peers, cultural metaphors.
Hooked-loop, BJ Fogg, Octalysis — applied for Uzbek 10-14yo. Mahalla / sinfdosh leaderboards beat Duolingo's anonymous global league at this market. Khorezmi-Beruniy-Ulug'bek prestige tiers beat American fantasy avatars. Streak with repair mechanic (loss aversion without cruelty). Surprise "Beruniy cards" at 1-in-5. No gem-pulling, no gacha, parents are sensitive to qimor.
Pillar 05 · The journey has an end
Supernova → Zij catalog → Galaxy.
Today the "done" card is a flat string. After igniting all stars in a constellation: nothing happens. Build the chapter-end cinematic — a star collapses into a named, dated supernova ("Bobur'ning supernovasi · 2026-yil noyabr"). Permanent. The Personal Zij — the student's own catalog modeled on Ulug'bek's 1437 Samarqand zij — accumulates supernovas. Becomes the 5-year-veteran's permanent artifact.
Pillar 06 · Content production at scale
11 lessons today → 500 lessons.
A 5-step LLM-assisted pipeline: (1) curriculum graph entry → (2) LLM card-deck draft (JSON-schema constrained) → (3) automatic checks (python solver + pint units + multi-model voting + pedagogy-arc lint) → (4) Uzbek teacher review (Khanmigo-Writing-Coach pattern) → (5) AB telemetry release. Target: 1 dev-day per lesson at 90% QC pass. ~$11/lesson all-in, 97% of which is human review, not tokens.

Month 1

The real mastery loop · BKT + half-life + Mastery Challenges.

15 dev-days
Goal: the star ignites because the child mastered the concept in mixed context, not because they tapped through the cards. The next lesson is picked at P(correct) ≈ 0.85. The forgetting curve is fought, not ignored.
2.1
Atomize the Grade-6 curriculum into ~200 skills.
Take the 54 textbook topics and split each into 3–6 atomic skills — the smallest unit a Grade-6 student could plausibly fail in isolation. Output: a JSON DAG at lib/curriculum/skills.json with prerequisite edges. Each skill carries: {id, domain, unit, prereqs[], stockProblems[], conceptTag}. Skills are tagged with priors P(G)=0.20, P(S)=0.08, P(T)=0.18 from the literature.
new: skills.json (200 entries)effort: 4 days (one-time)
2.2
BKT + half-life recall — the mastery schema.
Firestore: users/{uid}/mastery/{skillId} with {correct, total, streak, halfLifeDays, lastSeen, nextDue, pL, pT, pG, pS, mastery, level, lastAttempts[10], prereqsOf[]}. On every attempt: BKT update of pL, half-life update (correct/no-hint → ×1.7, correct/hint → ×1.2, wrong → ×0.5, floor 1 day), composite mastery = pL · R(now), level threshold {0.5/0.8/0.95}. Schedule nextDue = lastSeen + halfLife · log₂(1/0.85). FIRe-style fractional credits to prereqs (weight 0.4).
new: lib/mastery.tseffort: 3 daysimpact: load-bearing
2.3
Mixed-context Mastery Challenge — Khan's signature surface.
A new card type: mastery-challenge. Renders 4–6 items pulled from different skills the student recently practiced. Same-skill grinding can never grant mastered — only the Challenge can. This is what makes mastery retrieved, not crammed. Triggers every 5 lessons within a constellation, and weekly on dormant skills (the spaced-repetition surface).
new: MasteryChallenge.tsxeffort: 3 days
2.4
The session scheduler — 55/25/10/10 mix at P(correct) = 0.85.
A new lib/scheduler.ts that, given a user and a target session length, returns a sequence of cards picked by: 55% ZPD core (predicted P(correct) ∈ [0.75, 0.90]), 25% at-risk review (predicted recall ≤ 0.85, weighted by prereq fan-out), 10% exploratory (new topic at the prereq frontier OR mixed-context), 10% confidence builder (recently-mastered, P(correct) ≥ 0.95). Hard cap: 3 consecutive items per skill to force interleaving. Three wrongs in a row → drop one level on the prereq graph (remediation mode).
new: lib/scheduler.tseffort: 3 days
2.5
Per-topic mastery percentage visible on every star.
Render mastery on every StarTile. Constellation total becomes "27% of Burj mastered" instead of "3 of 6 lessons done." Single highest-density "this app knows me" signal in EdTech — the number visibly changes as the student practices.
touches: StarTile.tsxeffort: 1 day
2.6
Personal Zij at /osmonim/zij.
A scrollable, dated catalog of every star + supernova the student has ignited, styled like Ulug'bek's 1437 Samarqand zij. Shareable URL. Auto-backfills every existing user's history. Becomes the "5-year veteran" artifact the moment we ship — and the export anchor for the parent weekly digest.

Month 2–3

Humo = Koji-grade · the in-canvas AI tutor.

25 dev-days
Goal: Humo doesn't sit in a chat sidebar. Humo sees the lens, the lever, the circuit. Humo draws on it. On every page arrival, Humo says something specific to what changed since the last card. On a wrong answer, Humo never says "wrong" — it says "I got a different answer, walk me through your step."
3.1
Sim state contract — every sim publishes a structured state object.
Add a useSimState(state, schema) hook to every sim component. The hook publishes {simKey, params, observations} to a context provider. LensSim publishes {focalLength, objectDistance, imagePosition, studentMarkedFocal}. CircuitSim publishes {voltage, resistance, current, isClosed}. Humo can now read what the student is looking at.
touches: all simseffort: 4 days
3.2
Canvas write primitives — humo.highlight(), humo.annotate(), humo.poseSubProblem().
A registry of tools Humo can call in the sim canvas. The LLM is instructed (via function-calling / tool-use) to prefer a canvas action over a sentence. When a student gets a lens question wrong, Humo calls highlight("focal-point") + annotate(x, y, "this distance equals f") instead of typing a paragraph. This is the architectural advantage over Khanmigo.
new: lib/humo/tools.tseffort: 5 daysimpact: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
3.3
The Humo system prompt — the Khanmigo kernel + Koji canvas + Uzbek voice.
A constrained system prompt with hard rules: (1) never give the final answer, (2) one question per turn, (3) end every reply with a question, (4) on wrong answer say "I got a different answer" not "wrong", (5) after 3 help requests without effort, zoom out and ask which part of the previous hint they missed, (6) reading level no higher than the student's, (7) prefer canvas action over sentence, (8) one variable warm Uzbek voice — kind older sibling, not bouncy American. Injects {currentLesson, currentCardId, cardKind, expectedAnswer, studentLastAttempt, simState, masterySnapshot} per turn.
new: lib/humo/prompt.tseffort: 3 days iteration
3.4
Page-arrival narration — Humo says something specific on every new card.
When the deck advances to a new card, fire a low-latency Humo call with {prevSimState, newCardContext} and request a single sentence: "Sen oxirgi safar fokus masofasini 4 sm ga sozlading — endi linzaning kuchi qancha?" This is Koji's killer move. Costs ~$0.001 per card transition with Sonnet. Cache by {cardId, simStateHash} so common transitions are free after first hit.
touches: LessonDeck.tsxeffort: 2 days
3.5
Mistake-driven branching — the "different answer" move.
When the student submits a wrong MCQ/numeric answer, the renderer does NOT show the red-X. Instead it calls Humo with {question, expected, given} and asks: "Generate a question that leads the student to discover their error WITHOUT announcing it. Start with 'I got a different answer — walk me through how you got X.'" Hint laddering: rung 1 = question, rung 2 = sub-problem, rung 3 = highlight on canvas, rung 4 = explicit nudge. Never the answer.
touches: McqCard, NumericCardeffort: 4 days
3.6
Voice — Humo speaks Uzbek with optional Russian.
Gemini Live or GPT-Realtime end-to-end speech tokens (warmer than STT→LLM→TTS). Voice-first only on Grade 6 onboarding screens and the "explain back to me" card type. Optional everywhere else. Persona: warm-formal older brother / ustoz, not bubbly American. iOS Safari audio-unlock-on-first-tap (we already handle this in SoundSim).
new: lib/humo/voice.tseffort: 4 daysimpact: huge for <10yo
3.7
Humo character interludes — Ibn Sino / Beruniy / Ulug'bek / Khorezmi mode.
Khanmigo's killer feature is debate-with-Lincoln. Humo's localized version: chat with Ibn Sino about lenses (after the optics lesson), with Beruniy about astronomy (after eclipses), with Khorezmi about algebra. Persona prompts written in collaboration with an Uzbek literature reviewer. Default to Uzbek historical figures, not Western canon. Lincoln/Newton available as electives.
touches: lib/personas.tseffort: 3 days

Quarter 2

The content engine · from 11 to 500 lessons.

60 dev-days · 1 dev + 1 Uzbek teacher reviewer
Goal: produce 1 new lesson per dev-day with 90% QC pass rate. By end of quarter: Grade-6 fully covered (~70 lessons), Grade-7 math + physics started (~50 more), Grade-11 DTM prep started (~20 algebra/physics). Total 150+ by Q2 close, on the way to 500 by Y1.
4.1
JSON-schema-constrained LLM card-deck draft.
A pipeline that takes {skillId, gradeLevel, prereqsMastered} and emits a full lesson card-deck JSON matching the 12-card DSL with a hard schema constraint enforcing the pedagogy arc (hook → teach → sim → consolidate → ask → done). For numeric problems, emits a python_solver field alongside the answer. Uses Sonnet 4.6 + structured outputs. Cost: ~$0.08 / draft.
new: scripts/author-lesson.tseffort: 5 days
4.2
Automatic QC — 5 layers before a teacher sees it.
A CI job that runs on every authored deck: (1) Python solver runs and matches stated answer, (2) pint dimensional analysis on every numeric answer, (3) multi-model re-solve — Claude + GPT vote, flag any disagreement, (4) CODATA constant lookup on all named physical constants, (5) pedagogy-arc lint — rejects decks where simulation precedes explainer. Catches ~95% of LLM hallucinations before human review.
new: .github/workflows/qc.ymleffort: 4 days
4.3
Teacher review surface — the Khanmigo Writing Coach pattern.
A new /admin/review route for Uzbek physics + math teachers. Reviewer sees: rendered deck preview, the LLM's chain-of-thought, all auto-check results, side-by-side English translation. Approve / edit / reject with one-click. Edits feed back as future-shot examples for the authoring LLM — the corpus improves on its own. Pay teachers per approved lesson, not per hour ($5–8 / lesson).
new: app/admin/revieweffort: 6 days
4.4
12–20 reusable sim components — the multiplier.
The 13 sims today are scenarios. Refactor into parameterized components: <PendulumSim length={1.2} g={9.8} damping={0.02}>, <CircuitSim topology="series" components={[...]}>, <WaveSim wavelength={500} amplitude={0.5}>, <CollisionSim m1={2} v1={3} m2={5} v2={-1} elastic={true}>. Now any new lesson is JSON pointing at a shelf, not new TypeScript. Brilliant ships at scale because their sim shelf has ~60 components.
refactor: sims/effort: 15 daysimpact: enables 4.5
4.5
A/B telemetry release pipeline.
Every newly approved lesson ships to 5% of matched-grade learners. After 100 sessions, compare against the median lesson on {median time-on-card, retry rate, drop-off, sentiment-after}. Auto-promote at +0σ, auto-flag at –1σ. Re-author queue is fed by the threshold rule. Lesson quality compounds.
touches: Firebase Remote Configeffort: 4 days
4.6
Add 4 missing card types — the SOTA card grammar.
From the audit vs Perseus + Duolingo: (a) numeric-input with units — free-form numeric answer parsed by pint, (b) graph-mark — click to place a point on axes (the "leap year searches" pattern from /scrn/), (c) free-body-diagram — drag force arrows onto an object, (d) predict-then-show — learner sets a slider prediction, sim then runs and reveals truth. The obsession ladder · time scales of return

Why the child comes back tomorrow, next week, next year.

Every successful learning app stacks rewards at four time scales — immediate, daily, weekly, lifelong. Today Scorpius has one. The 5 phases above add the other three, in order. None of these are gamification spice — they're the structural reasons a 5-year veteran exists.

Seconds
Immediate · the dopamine micro-reward
Every correct answer fires a pentatonic chime, a particle burst, an XP tick. Variable-ratio (~70% small / 20% medium / 10% "wow"). The card lock + ignite lives here. Today: present, weak. Phase 1 multiplies it.
Days
Daily · the streak + Bugungi yulduz
A real streak with repair mechanic. One "Bugungi yulduz" nominated lesson with 2× ignition. Push notification at the right hour. Loss aversion does the work. Today: hardcoded to 1. Phase 1 ships the real version.
Weeks
Weekly · sinfdosh league + parent Telegram
Class leaderboard, mahalla guild, weekly demotion / promotion. Parent gets the Telegram brief every Sunday. Schools light up. Social weight beats the daily by 10×. Today: absent. Phase 5 ships it.
Lifelong
Lifelong · the Zij, the supernovas, the Galaxy
The Personal Zij is the artifact a 5-year veteran has and a newcomer doesn't. Supernovas are dated and named. Beruniy / Ibn Sino / Ulug'bek tiers visibly change the night sky. Today: void. Phase 2 ships it.

IX
What we do not build · cuts of conscience

Three temptations rejected.

Almost everything you can imagine for a learning platform shows up somewhere on the obsession spectrum. These three are off-limits — either because they corrupt the brand, fail culturally, or burn dev-days for no compounding return.

RejectedWhy
Gem-pulling / lockbox / gacha visuals Parents read it as qimor. Multiple jurisdictions now classify minor-targeted variable-rate cosmetic drops with explicit rarity tiers as gambling. Variable-ratio rewards stay (XP, mastery jumps, surprise unlocks) — but no spinning wheels, no "open the chest," no rarity %s.
Pivot to student-pay first At 80K/oy, realistic payer mix is 85% parent / 5% self-pay. Teen card penetration under 18 is ~3%. Switching messaging to "students pay" collapses LTV from 1.9M to <500K. The student is the user; the parent is the buyer. Both surfaces, one subscription. (Optional 25K student tier as hedge, never the headline.)
American-fantasy avatars / wizards / dragons Cultural alien. Replace with: scientist-in-the-observatory, alchemist's lab, geometry-pattern quest in Silk Road imagery. If we do avatars at all, do'ppi + Samarqand tile patterns, not robes.
Streak guilt notifications ("Duo is sad") Tashkent reads it as manipulative. Notification copy must be warm: "Aziz aka, bugun ham birga o'qiymizmi?" not "Your streak is dying."
Generic global leaderboards Western "you vs 50M strangers" is weaker than "your class vs 6-B." Lean named, knowable, mahalla-scoped. Cross-class tournaments beat global by far in this market.
DKT or transformer KT before Phase 5 Cold-start problem dominates at <10K attempts per skill cluster. BKT is interpretable, ships in days, performs within 5% of DKT until you have real volume. Defer the deep model.
