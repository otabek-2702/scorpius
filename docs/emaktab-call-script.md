## Part 1 — The phone call (target: 6–8 minutes total)

### 1.1 Opening — first 30 seconds (memorize this verbatim)

> "Assalomu alaykum. Mening ismim **Murodjon Tolipov**. Men **Scorpius** loyihasi asoschisiman —
> emaktab.uz o'quvchilari uchun **AI o'qituvchi platforma** quryapmiz. Sizning vaqtingiz
> bo'lsa, qisqa hamkorlik gaplashishni xohlardim. 5 daqiqa beradigan vaqtingiz bormi?"

**If they say "Nima haqida?" → continue with §1.2.**
**If they say "Hozir banman" → "Tushunaman. Sizga qaysi vaqt qulay bo'ladi? Bugun yoki ertaga
1-2 daqiqa." Get a callback time. Don't push.**
**If they say "Bizga shu kabi takliflar ko'p keladi" → "Tushunaman. Lekin biz bugun **GDG
EdTech Hakathonida** finalga chiqdik va **Farg'ona viloyat Maktabgacha va Maktab ta'lim
boshqarmasi** bilan ham hamkorlik qilyapmiz. Faqat 3 daqiqa." (use the credibility lever)**

### 1.2 The hook — next 60 seconds

> "Biz **Farg'ona viloyat Maktabgacha va Maktab ta'lim boshqarmasi** bilan birgalikda 6-sinf
> o'quvchilari uchun **shaxsiy AI o'qituvchi** quryapmiz.
>
> Ishlash printsipi juda oddiy: o'quvchi yoki ota-onaning **roziligi bilan** uning emaktab
> hisobiga ulanamiz. Baholarni o'qiymiz, qaysi mavzu qiyin kelyaptini topamiz, va o'sha mavzu
> bo'yicha **8 daqiqalik shaxsiy darsni o'zbek tilida** beramiz.
>
> Hozir **demo tayyor**, 10 nafar o'quvchi sinab ko'rdi, ko'rsatkichlar yaxshi.
>
> Bugun **soat 12 da GDG hakatonida** rasmiy ko'rsatamiz va kelajak qadamlar haqida
> gaplashamiz. Sizning **rasmiy bilimingiz** bizning loyiha uchun juda muhim — siz bizning
> ekotizimning poydevorisiz."

**Notice what this does:**
- "Birgalikda" = together (frames as partnership not request)
- "Roziligi bilan" = with consent (establishes legal basis up front)
- "Sizning rasmiy bilimingiz" = your formal awareness (a soft ask for "no objection")
- Names a real event (GDG hackathon) + real partner (Fergana) — both are checkable, both are true

### 1.3 The narrow ask — 30 seconds

> "Sizdan ikkita kichik narsa so'ramoqchiman:
>
> **Birinchi:** bugun **bilingki**, biz emaktab.uz nomini hurmat bilan eslatib, foydalanuvchi
> roziligi bilan ishlayapmiz. Hech qanday avtomatik ma'lumot olish, hech qanday tijoriy
> sotish yo'q.
>
> **Ikkinchi:** kelasi haftada **30 daqiqalik uchrashuv** — rasmiy hamkorlik shakli qanday
> bo'lishi mumkinligi haqida gaplashamiz. Sizning vaqtingizga moslashtirib."

**Wait for response. Don't fill silence.**

### 1.4 Branching responses

#### If they say YES / "Yaxshi, gaplashamiz"

> "Rahmat. Demak, **bugun pitch'imda** sizning ismingizdan e'tirozsizlik borligini ayta
> olamanmi? Yoki faqat **'emaktab bilan suhbat boshlandi'** desam bo'ladimi?"

(They'll almost always pick the second — "say we're in conversation." That's a WIN.)

Then: "Iltimos, **Telegram username yoki email** bering — rasmiy xat va demo havolasini
yuboraman. Sizning rasmiy tasdig'ingiz bilan."

**EXTRACT:**
- [ ] Telegram OR email
- [ ] Their full name + position (for "I spoke with X, Y department")
- [ ] Permission to mention them in pitch (any form)
- [ ] Best time for the 30-min meeting next week

#### If they say "WAIT / We need to review / Need legal opinion"

> "Albatta, tushunaman. Bu sizning **professional ehtiyotkorligingiz** — biz uni hurmat
> qilamiz. Bugun **men yo'q narsani da'vo qilmayman**. Faqat suhbat boshladik deyman.
>
> Sizga bugun **rasmiy xat** yuborib, hujjatlarni ko'rib chiqishingiz uchun vaqt beramen.
> Kelasi hafta qulay vaqtda gaplashsak bo'ladi. Sizga **Telegram orqali yuborayinmi yoki
> email orqali?**"

**This still wins** because you can honestly say in pitch: "We're in formal conversation with
emaktab.uz — letter sent today, review in progress."

#### If they say NO / "We're against this / Stop"

(unlikely but prepare)

> "Tushundim. Iltimos, **aniq qaysi qism** sizni xavotirga solyapti? Foydalanuvchi
> roziligimi, ma'lumot xavfsizligimi, biznes modelimi? **Men o'rganishni xohlayman**, chunki
> bizning maqsadimiz **sizga qarshi emas, balki sizning tizimingizni qo'llab-quvvatlash**."

(Listen. Don't argue. Their objection becomes your roadmap.)

After they explain: "Rahmat, bu juda muhim. **Bugun pitch'da hech narsa aytmaymen**. Kelasi
hafta rasmiy uchrashuvga taklif qilsam bo'ladimi — bu masalalarni birga ko'rib chiqamiz?"

Even a NO becomes a "we paused our launch out of respect" story — which is itself credible.

#### If they say "WHO TOLD YOU TO CALL US?"

> "Hech kim. Bu **bizning tashabbusimiz** — biz emaktab.uz ni hurmat qilamiz va siz bilan
> **birinchi bo'lib gaplashishni** to'g'ri deb topdik. Aks holda, biz sizning bilimingizsiz
> ishlasaida bo'lardi — lekin biz **shaffof yo'lni** tanladik."

(This is a HUGE positive frame — they will respect this.)

### 1.5 Soft close (regardless of outcome)

> "Vaqtingiz uchun chin yurakdan rahmat. Bugun **rasmiy xat** yuboraman — sizning
> ko'rib chiqishingiz uchun. Ko'rishguncha."

## Part 2 — Practical call hygiene

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Have a glass of water ready | Don't call from a noisy place |
| Smile while talking — they hear it | Don't say "scrape", "extract", "pull data" — use **"o'qiymiz" / "ulanamiz" / "ma'lumot olish"** |
| Pause after questions; let silence work | Don't fill awkward silence with extra promises |
| Take notes — name, position, exact words | Don't ask anything you can't verify |
| Use `siz` form throughout, never `sen` | Don't say "we're already doing this" — say "biz tayyor / hozir tayyorlanmoqdamiz" |
| Mention Fergana + GDG once, not twice | Don't lie about partnerships you don't have |
| End in <8 min — respect their time | Don't ask for funding, signups, anything beyond no-objection + meeting |

## Part 3 — Pitch Q&A defense pack

These are the questions that can crack you on stage. **Memorize the 2-sentence answers.**

### Q1: "How do you get the data from emaktab? Is this legal?"

**Best answer (Uzbek):**
> "Foydalanuvchi o'zining roziligi bilan o'zining emaktab kirish ma'lumotlarini bizga beradi —
> xuddi 'Sign in with Google' kabi. Biz faqat **o'sha foydalanuvchining o'zining ma'lumotini**
> shifrlangan tarzda olib, **o'sha foydalanuvchining o'zi uchun** ishlatamiz. Bugun ertalab
> emaktab.uz vakili bilan **rasmiy suhbat boshladik** — ular bizning yondashuvimizdan
> xabardor."

**Best answer (English):**
> "The student or parent authorizes us with their own emaktab credentials — same model as
> 'Sign in with Google'. We only fetch **that user's own data** for **that user's own
> personalization**. We are in formal conversation with emaktab.uz as of this morning —
> they're aware of our approach."

### Q2: "What if emaktab.uz sues you?"

> "Hozir emaktab.uz bilan to'g'ridan-to'g'ri **hamkorlik suhbati** olib boryapmiz. Ularga
> bugun rasmiy xat yubordik. Bizning yondashuvimiz **shaffof va o'quvchi roziligiga
> asoslangan** — bu bizni qonuniy himoyaga oladi. Bundan tashqari, **Farg'ona viloyat
> Maktabgacha va Maktab ta'lim boshqarmasi** bilan ham hamkorlikdamiz, ya'ni davlat
> tomonidan ham tanilgan loyiha."

### Q3: "What stops emaktab from just building this themselves?"

> "Hech narsa — va biz buni **hush ko'ramiz**. Bizning maqsadimiz raqobat emas, balki
> **bo'shliqni to'ldirish**. Agar ular shu featureni qo'shsalar — biz **rasmiy partner**
> bo'lib qolamiz. Agar ular qo'ymasalar — biz mustaqil shaxsiy o'qituvchi sifatida davom
> etamiz. Ikkala variant ham **o'zbek o'quvchisi uchun foyda**."

### Q4: "Why won't emaktab block your IP / disable your scraping?"

> "Biz **scraping** qilmayapmiz. Har bir foydalanuvchi **o'zining brauzerida o'zining
> hisobiga kiradi** — bu emaktab uchun oddiy o'quvchi sessiyasi. Texnik tomondan
> bloklash imkoni yo'q, chunki biz **alohida foydalanuvchi sifatida** ulanamiz.
> Yana — biz **emaktab nomidan** muloqotda turibmiz, ular bizning vositamizni biladi."

### Q5: "How do you store passwords? What about a data breach?"

> "Parollar **never** ochiq saqlanmaydi. **AES-256 shifrlash** bilan saqlaymiz va faqat
> **o'sha foydalanuvchining o'z ma'lumotini** olish uchun ishlatamiz. Hech kim — hatto
> biz ham — boshqa foydalanuvchining ma'lumotiga ko'ra olmaymiz. **Bug bounty** ham
> ochamiz launch'dan keyin."

### Q6: "What's your terms-of-service / privacy policy?"

> "Rasmiy hujjatlarimiz **launch'dan oldin** tayyor bo'ladi — hozir mavjud draft —
> O'zbekiston qonunchiligi va GDPR me'yorlariga muvofiq. Foydalanuvchi har doim **o'z
> ma'lumotini olib tashlashi** yoki **hisobni o'chirishi** mumkin."

### Q7: "Who's on your team? What's your experience?"

> "Men Murodjon — backend va sistem dizayni. **Amirsaid** — product va biznes.
> **Zaynobiddin** — CTO, infrastruktura. Hozir 3 nafarmiz, kelasi oyda 2 yana qo'shamiz —
> contentredaktori va Uzbek pedagog. Bizning oldingi loyihamiz **Humopedia** — 1 yil
> ichida 10K+ foydalanuvchiga ega bo'ldi."

### Q8: "What if a 15-year-old kid signs up without parental consent?"

> "13 yoshdan kichik foydalanuvchilar uchun **ota-ona elektron rozilik talab qilinadi**.
> Onboardingda yosh tasdiqlanadi va kichik yoshli foydalanuvchilarda **ota-onaning telefon
> raqami majburiy**. Bu O'zbekiston **'Shaxsiy ma'lumotlar to'g'risida'gi qonunining**
> 14-moddasiga mos keladi."

### Q9: "Why Uzbek and not Russian? Don't you exclude Russian-speaking kids?"

> "Yo'q — biz **o'zbek tilini ustun** qilamiz, chunki shu yerda **bo'shliq eng katta**.
> Russian content YouTube'da yetarli. Lekin **Russian + Karakalpak** ham roadmap'da bor —
> Q4 2026."

### Q10: "How will you scale technically? AI is expensive."

> "Bizning **kesh qatlami** har bir AI chaqiruvini hash bilan saqlaydi. **Bir o'quvchi
> to'laydi — qolgan barcha o'quvchilar bepul oladi**. Bu shuni anglatadiki, 100K
> foydalanuvchidagi marginal cost = 10 foydalanuvchidagi cost. Boshqa edtech
> startuplar bunday qura olmaydi."
